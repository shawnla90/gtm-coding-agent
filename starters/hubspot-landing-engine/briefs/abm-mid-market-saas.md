## What I'd build for {{company_name}}

A four-stage engine that lives next to your existing stack and writes back to
it. Pull. Enrich. Reason. Write. Same pattern that already wired your CRM to
your terminal in Chapter 13. Now extended so every qualified contact has a
matching landing page, not just three custom-property angles.

The build looks like this:

1. **Pull** — qualified contacts via your current MQL definition, no schema
   changes. Pulls from the system you already use.
2. **Enrich** — Apollo for the things that move the angle. Company size, CRM,
   email provider, hiring signals. About 5 minutes per 50 contacts.
3. **Reason** — Claude subagents on Opus-tier models, one per insight. Each
   subagent has a single objective. Quality up, latency down, cost
   predictable.
4. **Write** — your CRM and your CMS at the same time. Custom properties on
   the contact record, a personalized landing page at a stable URL, and a
   tracking event so your SDR sees both before the first send.

The deliverable is the pattern, not a one-off campaign. Once it runs, the
same engine handles next quarter's launch with one config swap.

## Why this beats the SaaS approach

The model tier matters. {{model_tier_wedge}}. Most "AI for sales" tools wrap
generic models because they have to amortize across thousands of customers.
You control the list. You can afford the top tier. That asymmetry is the
whole reason this play works.
